# Confidence Model

## High

Primary evidence directly records the event or activity, identity and timestamps are reliable, and little inference is required.

## Medium

Multiple signals support the conclusion, but evidence is incomplete or interpretation is required.

## Low

Evidence is indirect, sparse, or mostly self-reported, and credible alternatives remain.

## Unknown

The evidence cannot support the claim.

Confidence is not a numerical probability. Overall report quality must not hide weak finding-level confidence.
