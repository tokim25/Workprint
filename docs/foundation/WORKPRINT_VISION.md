# Workprint Vision

Status: Durable vision
Purpose: Defines Workprint's mission, audience, success, and long-term direction
Expected Update Frequency: Rarely changes

## Mission

Workprint helps people understand how work came to be by reconstructing the
available evidence into a clear, traceable account of activity, decisions,
collaboration, confidence, and unknowns.

It exists for a world where meaningful work is increasingly made across humans,
AI systems, documents, design tools, repositories, and conversations. The
history of that work is often scattered. Workprint turns that scattered record
into an accountable narrative without pretending to know more than the evidence
allows.

## Problem Statement

Modern project history is fragmented. A product decision may begin in a chat,
continue in a document, appear in a design file, become code in a repository,
and be summarized later in a report. AI tools can accelerate each step, but
they also make it harder for an outside reader to understand who directed the
work, what was produced, which choices mattered, and which claims are actually
supported.

Most project artifacts show outputs, not provenance. They rarely explain the
sequence of decisions, the role of human judgment, the contribution of tools,
or the gaps in the record. As a result, people are left with two bad options:
trust a polished story without evidence, or inspect raw logs without a story.

Workprint exists to create a third option: a careful reconstruction that is
readable, useful, and bounded by evidence.

## Why Workprint Exists

Workprint exists because trust in work increasingly depends on understanding
the process behind it. A hiring manager may want to evaluate judgment. A client
may want to understand scope and diligence. A teammate may need project
continuity. A legal reviewer may need attribution boundaries. A creator may
want to share their work without overstating what the record proves.

Workprint serves all of those needs by separating captured evidence from
unsupported certainty. It can show that a user directed a milestone, that an AI
tool implemented a requested change, that a document lacks revision history,
or that a decision is visible only indirectly. It can make confidence legible.
It can make unknowns respectable.

## Who It Serves

Workprint serves project creators who need a fair account of their work. It
serves reviewers who need to understand process rather than inspect only final
outputs. It serves clients and teammates who need continuity. It serves
auditors and legal reviewers who care about evidence boundaries. It serves
organizations trying to make human-AI collaboration accountable without
turning every report into surveillance or speculation.

Workprint should be usable by people with limited coding knowledge. Its long
term product should not assume that a user is comfortable with terminal
commands, source-specific export formats, or implementation internals.

## What Success Looks Like

Workprint succeeds when a reader can understand:

- what project was investigated;
- what evidence was reviewed;
- what outputs and milestones the evidence supports;
- how the project appears to have evolved;
- where human direction, AI/tool activity, collaborator activity, joint
  activity, and unattributed activity appear in the captured record;
- which decisions mattered and how strongly they are supported;
- what Workprint cannot know from the available evidence;
- why the report's confidence level is appropriate.

Success also means that a skeptical reader can follow the path from conclusion
to evidence. A good Workprint report should reduce the need for blind trust.

## What Workprint Must Never Become

Workprint must never become a contribution scoreboard. It must not calculate
human-versus-AI percentages, infer ownership from volume, or treat message
counts, token counts, commits, or edit size as proxies for value.

It must never become a laundering mechanism for unsupported authorship claims.
It should not make a project look more human-led, more AI-led, more complete,
or more certain than the evidence supports.

It must never become a report generator that prioritizes confident prose over
traceable truth. Narrative matters, but narrative is in service of
understanding. It cannot be allowed to outrun evidence.

## Long-Term Vision

In the long term, Workprint should become a trusted provenance layer for
human-AI work. It should help users gather evidence from many sources, preview
what can be investigated, generate reports that nontechnical readers can
understand, and preserve source boundaries throughout.

The product should grow from command-line workflows toward low-code and
no-code experiences. It should support more evidence sources, richer project
timelines, better evidence coverage analysis, and clearer report design. It
should remain deterministic where possible and cautious where inference is
introduced.

The lasting ambition is simple: make the history of work easier to understand
without making it less honest.
